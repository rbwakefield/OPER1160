Business Planning Process II

Summary and References

The Business Planning Process is used to implement the Strategic Business Plan in an organization. Strategic Business Planning, Sales and Operations Planning, Master Production Scheduling, Material Requirements Planning and Production Activity Control work as coordinating mechanisms between functional areas to work towards the goals and objectives of the company. We saw how each level varies in purpose, level of detail, time span, with capacity checks and balances. These processes can also be applied effectively to service organizations.

As you complete the course material, we hope you have found that Supply and Operations Management is a critical part of every business’s goal of providing products or services for customers. It is important that all business students have an understanding of the Supply Chain concepts in these 8 Units so they can provide value to their future or current employer. These concepts, which are based on the APICS Body Of Knowledge (BOK), provides a basis for Supply Chain and Operations Diploma and Degree programs at numerous Colleges and Universities not only in North America, but worldwide.
