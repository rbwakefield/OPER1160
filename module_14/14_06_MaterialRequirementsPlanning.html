Business Planning Process II

Material Requirements Planning (MRP)

At this stage, we are planning the orders for manufactured and purchased components (dependent demand items) used in the production of finished saleable products (independent demand items).

Click for a larger version

Figure 1: MRP Inputs and Output. Adapted from Lopers pg 20 (2009)

MRP is a planning tool used to perform such detailed calculations. There is a shorter time bucket now--days or shifts. It uses a Bill of Material (BOM) for finished product. The three essential components required for the MRP calculation are shown in Figure 4. What happens if the inputs are not accurate? Garbage in, garbage out!

Required Inputs:

  1. Master Production Schedule (MPS)– realistic / feasible projection of planned finished goods
  2. Bill-of-Materials (BOM)- listing of components, parts and sub-assemblies and the quantities used to produce a finished good
  3. Inventory record / Open Orders / Planning Factors – must be accurate
    • Inventory record – amount of inventory on-hand
    • Open orders- what has already been started in house or committed to a supplier
    • Planning Factors- lead time, lot size, safety stock, scrap factors

If the MRP states we require 120 of item A and we have 40 in opening inventory and 100 previously ordered, there are 140 available. An additional order may not have to be placed given the other planning factors.

Output of the MRP

The output of the MRP is called a planned order which details what, when and how much to order. This planned order is verified with current information, then either goes straight to production (PAC) if the materials or already in-house, or to purchasing for ordering. A sample MRP grid, including a description of how the calculations are done, can be found in the Custom Chapter on pgs. 21 & 22. (Lopers, T., 2009, p. 21-22)
