Business Planning Process II

S & OP for Services

Services cannot be built ahead of time and stored in inventory but S&OP planning is still required to balance supply and demand.

There are two strategic options for S&OP in services.

Influencing Sales to Match Available Capacity

Capacity is fixed and cannot be change so you need to think about how sales may be “influenced” by appointments, discounts and promotions, seasonal complements and/or yield management.

Can you name a couple of service organizations in each category?

Appointments
doctor’s office, hair salon, vehicle repairs
Discounts and promotions
restaurants, retail
Seasonal complements
sell snowmobiles in the winter and seadoos in the summer, lawncare in the summer and snow removal in the winter
Yield management (adjusting prices to maximize profit)
hotels, airlines, concerts
Changing Capacity to Match Sales

Capacity in services is represented by the amount of labour available in a certain time period. To change capacity when it gets busy, we have to adjust the availability of that labour. Capacity can be changed to match the sales demand by cross training employees, shifting work to the customer or paying overtime, hiring part-time, temporary or seasonal workers. Using overtime is not a long term option due to increasing costs from premium wages and productivity losses.

Can you name a couple of service organizations in each of these categories?

Cross training employees
grocery or drug stores—workers who restock shelves are trained to operate cash registers when the lines get too long
Overtime in the short term
College bookstore at start of semester, restaurants/bars on special occasions, accountants during tax season
Hiring part time, temporary or seasonal workers
retail, landscaping, roofing, picking crops
Shifting work to the customer
grocery self-checkout, on-line banking, airport check-in, ATM, self-serve gas stations, buffets

It is important to realize that these production (level or chase) and adapting sales or capacities for services are decisions made at the S&OP level. We will now focus on the more detailed Planning Processes which are used mainly in production but can be adapted for services.
