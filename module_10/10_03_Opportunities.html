Supply Management II

Opportunities in an Integrated Supply Chain

Accurate “Pull Data” refers to having visibility throughout the entire supply chain by using Point-Of-Sale (POS) data to update the computerized planning system. Used correctly, everyone in the supply chain can then see what the customer is buying in real time and plan accordingly.

A reduction in lot size, how many the retailer or customer orders at a time, cuts inventory carrying costs and ordering lead time. Combined with better inventory visibility by using accurate pull data, overall supply chain inventory is reduced as well as the response time to changing customer ordering patterns. (cost reduction and responsiveness strategies from Unit # 2). This reduces surprises in the supply chain.

Collaborative Planning, Forecasting, and Replenishment (CPFR) is where the whole supply chain shares information (forecasts) and collaborates on a single plan to manage inventory throughout. This synchronizes the timing and quantity of flows and reduces surprises in the supply chain. Product promotions no longer serve as disturbances with customer satisfaction being the focus of all efforts.

Single Stage Control of Replenishment – Designating a member in the supply chain that is responsible for monitoring and managing inventory in the supply chain based on the “pull” from the end user. This is used very effectively by Wal-mart, combined with CPFR, to maintain their “retail supply chain leader” position. Many others in retail and production industries use Wal-Mart as a benchmark for their supply chains practices.

One of the results is that companies are often forging long-term relationships with suppliers and negotiating rather than using as much competitive bidding. This means more sharing of information, a stronger focus on quality and a look towards developing newer, more innovative products and processes together.

Vendor Managed Inventories (VMI) is when suppliers take responsibility for inventory at a customer site. It is a streamlined approach to inventory management and order fulfillment and can only be done with closer integration between members of the supply chain. (Datalliance, n.d.) They figure out what is needed, put it there, maintain the inventory level and bill the customer only when it is used.

Blanket Order: Confirmed long-term order by a buyer to a seller for supplying specified good(s) or service(s), for a fixed period or in a fixed quantity, at agreed-on prices or pricing method. After its acceptance by the supplier, supplies may be made against it periodically, on as-and-when-required basis, or as specified in the order, without calling for new purchase orders. source: businessdictionary.com

What if you decide that outsourcing is not for you and that more control over your supply chain should be a corporate priority? You could then decide to use Vertical Integration. That is the process by which you take control over other parts of your supply chain by purchasing or creating them.

A good example is a Canadian company that some of you might have heard of – Tim Horton’s. To gain control over their suppliers they Backward Integrated by buying the source of their baked goods (Bakerite Foods later bought by Maple Leaf Foods). To reduce variation in their delivery schedules, they Forward Integrated by building a distribution Centre and added a truck fleet in Guelph ON to serve eastern Canada. They now have control over supply, retail and distribution to better serve their customers. One of the reasons why Burger King bought Tim Horton’s was to use their efficient and effective Canadian Distribution network.
