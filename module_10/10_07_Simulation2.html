Supply Management II

Supply Chain Simulation

Objective

Realize the ideal supplier may not always have the lowest price per unit. There are many factors that are involved in selecting an ideal supplier. Deliver 25,000 gizmos and keep your procurement costs under $1,000,000.

Scenario

AJ Manufacturing just won their first large order of gizmos from Munson & Witt Inc. You are hired as a supply chain analyst to help AJ Manufacturing analyze and make supplier selection decisions for two critical components required for the Munson & Witt order. Your goal will be to maximize the profits of the order through minimizing the cost of procurement and ensuring that the order is delivered on time. Your goal is to deliver 25,000 gizmos and keep your procurement costs under $1m.

Learning Objectives

  • Remember the ideal supplier may not always have the lowest price per unit. There are many factors that are involved in selecting an ideal supplier.
  • Develop a better understanding of the factors that are involved in the supplier selection decision.
  • Realize that an ideal supplier may not be consistent across different types of items.
  • Many operations management decisions are complex problems with numerous areas of uncertainty.

Pointers

  • There is a lot of data to keep track of which may take you a couple rounds.
  • Do not start too slow or delivery delays later may result in you missing production targets.
  • TKeep an eye on the supplier capacity per week early.
  • TPay attention to your quarterly production targets as they are not all equal.
  • TYou can only place one order per supplier at a time.
  • TCompany reputations are important in regards to delivery delays and quality of the components.

Remember, you have 3 tries to perfect your inventory ordering.
