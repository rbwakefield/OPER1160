Quality

Quality and its impact on Company Strategy

Quality as defined by the American Society for Quality (ASQ) as “the totality of features and characteristics of a product or service that bears on its ability to satisfy stated or implied needs.”

Who’s needs? Ultimately the customers (user based) as they are the ones that “talk” with their money. However, others may talk about the quality of the product (features, better performance) or of the manufacturing process (it has no variances from original design in product or service). Which one is right? All of these are important or you won’t get a quality product or service!

It is interesting how quality has been the focal point of many companies. Those that defined customer quality expectations have certainly been able to match their quality with one of the three strategies for success (remember from Week #3?). The sound company Bose (differentiation), ZTE phones (low cost) and Dell (responsiveness) as well as the Arnold Palmer Hospital in the first vignette of this chapter (Lopers et al., 2016, p. 82) are all examples of companies that are succeeding with strategies based on providing quality.

What companies come to mind when you think of quality? Mine are Apple, Mercedes, BMW, Toyota, West Jet, and Disney.

Quality, or the lack of it, affects the entire supply chain right from the original suppliers through every step to the customer. To be successful, a company must institutionalize the organizational practices, establish the principles to follow, empower employees to “make it right”—all this can yield an effective organization with a competitive edge. It is not easy but is certainly worth it to win over repeat customers.

Two ways that quality can improve profitability is through sales gains AND/OR reducing costs. Think of a simple income statement:

Gross Sales
Less: Cost of Goods Sold
= Gross Profit
Less: Operating Expenses
= Net Profit

If we can increase Gross sales, profits go up or if we decrease the Cost of Goods Sold and/or Operating Expenses, the profits go up!

Exercise

Read the phrases below, consider your answers, and then click on the phrase to compare your thoughts to the correct responses.

Think of a high quality product like Bose speakers. Name three ways Bose can increase their profit by increasing its sales.

  • Improved response – satisfied with one BOSE product, you may buy a second item by the same company. Plus, tell other customers too!
  • Flexible pricing – stores can set the price
  • Improved reputation – this allows a company to differentiate its product and charge a higher price--customers will pay more for a reputable brand.

Name three ways a company that produces high quality products can reduce their costs.

  • Improved productivity – if the process is smooth and all the parts fit or the service process is well-understood and easily executed, outputs per inputs of time spent (remember Chapter 1) will increase.
  • Lower rework and scrap costs – if a company doesn’t have to repair, fix or repeat making a product or delivering a service the costs are lowered and more profit is made.
  • Lower warranty costs – if a product doesn’t need to be returned for warranty, the costs come from replacing the product, shipping, remanufacturing (if even possible) or scrapping are all avoided--and don’t forget the future lost sales too!
