Quality

Summary and References

In this unit, we learned that good quality is the cornerstone of almost all successful corporations. Good or great quality results in increased sales and decreased costs, both of which lead to long term corporate profitability.

Supply Chain and Operations Managements’ responsibility is to execute the products or services that they, in conjunction with the rest of the company, developed so they meet or exceed the quality and performance expectations of the customers. We looked at the four categories of costs (COQ) surrounding this important goal. We looked at some of the quality focused organizations and certifications/awards for this area. We also examined Total Quality Management (TQM) concepts, and the role of inspection for both products and services.

In our next unit, we will look at measuring and improving processes using some of the many TQM tools shown in this chapter.
