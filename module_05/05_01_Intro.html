Introduction

This unit, which is span multiple weeks, is focused on the various dimensions of quality. Quality is an essential element for improving operational performance. Managing quality assists in building successful competitive advantages using the strategies of differentiation, lower cost value and response. Improved quality can also result in increased profit, by either increasing sales (improved response, higher prices, and improved reputation) or by reducing costs. To accomplish this increase in quality, it costs money so we’ll look at the four categories of costs surrounding quality. Recognizing that quality is important we will look at external certifications for ISO and national quality awards presented annually by different organizations in both Canada and The United States.

We conclude this unit by looking at the components of Total Quality Management, the Lean philosophy around continuous improvement, and whether inspection is important.

Week 5 Learning Outcomes

4.1 Define quality and describe how it helps corporate strategy and improves profitability.

4.2 Explain the costs of quality.

4.3 Describe levels of quality standards, awards and institutions promoting quality standards.

4.4 Define total quality management (TQM) and the lean philosophy of continuous improvement.

4.5 Describe the concepts of an effective TQM program.

4.6 Discuss the value of inspection.

4.7 Describe how TQM is applied in services.
