National Quality Awards

Many organizations give out awards for quality.

However, a national manufacturing/service quality award is typically awarded by a government (or not-for-profits with government ties) to make its country’s businesses more competitive on a global scale. Remember the history of Operations from Week 2 and how quality just became important in North America in the ‘80s? Most of these were established in the late ‘80s or early ‘90s (Wikipedia, 2017).

Some to note are:

Canada

Canada Awards for Excellence by Excellence Canada (formerly National Quality Institute) (1989)

Japan

Deming Prize by the Union of Japanese Scientists and Engineers (JUSE) (since 1951).

United States

Malcolm Baldrige National Quality Award by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (1988)
