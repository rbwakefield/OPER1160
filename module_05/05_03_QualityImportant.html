Quality

Reasons Quality is Important

Besides increased profitability, quality is also important for:

  • Company Reputation – the reputation ultimately translates into sales figures and is important for the ongoing success of a company.
  • Product Liability – companies cannot breach the warranty implied or explicit for products or services and more and more Canadian courts are holding them liable. Have you ever checked out the Government of Canada “Recalls and Safety Alerts” website? The number of items listed are quite surprising.
  • Global Implications – companies are expanding globally and their quality does matter. Read this article on how mislabeled Canadian beef in a store in China shows how its poor quality could reflect badly on both the company, and in this case, Canada (Duckworth, 2015).

Costs of Quality (COQ)

Quality does cost money and we group the costs into four categories. In the American Society of Quality and industry they are known as Costs of Quality (COQ).

Prevention

Costs to reduce the potential for defect parts or services (training, quality improvement programs)

Appraisal

Evaluating (labs, inspectors)

Internal Failure

Costs due to defective products or processes
(rework, scrap, downtime)

External Costs

Costs that occur after the delivery (warranty, liabilities, returned goods, goodwill)

I would highly recommend you examine the two following additional (optional) resources to explain the Costs of Quality:

  1. Good article by the ASQ (American Standard of Quality) that explains these costs in detail. (ASQ, 2017)
  2. The Cost of Poor Quality (2:37) by SME that explain the 4 Costs of Quality. It does a good job of explaining the types of activities and costs for each of the first three categories.
