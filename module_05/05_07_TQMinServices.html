TQM in Services

The quality of the delivery of services is more difficult to measure than something that is tangible.

However, TQM in services is also important. In addition to regular inspections, service companies may use customer satisfaction surveys or hire Mystery Shoppers.

Reading

Read OM in Action: Richey International’s Spies…

…to see how this luxury hotel maintains quality. You can be sure they have Service Recovery strategies in place that train and empower workers to solve problems immediately.

Quality is often unspoken images in the purchaser’s mind. Yes, there is the tangible part of a service, like was the bill correct, but there are nine other determinants of service quality delivered in a service process (Lopers et al, 2017 p. 102).

Reliability
done right the first time and honours all promises.
Responsiveness
timeliness of service.
Competence
having the right skills and training.
Access
ease of contact.
Courtesy
politeness, respect.
Communication
informing customers in the language and at a level they understand.
Credibility
trustworthiness, honesty.
Security
freedom from danger, risk or doubt.
Understanding the customer
making an effort to know what a customer wants.
Tangible part
like the bill is correct.

It is important that the operations team work to ensure the tangible and intangible parts of any service
lives up to the customers’ expected level of quality.
