Operations Strategy in a Global Environment

Global View of Operations

Today's manager must have a global view of strategy.

Who has more worldwide restaurant sites than McDonald's?

The answer is Subway. Subway has about 25% more restaurants, but only one-third of the sales.

Rapid growth in world trade and emerging markets such as China and Eastern Europe means that many organizations can and often must extend both their operations and their customer base globally to remain competitive. Some Ontario based companies such as ATS Automation, have locations in Canada, the United Stated, Europe and Asia. International companies, like Rockwell Automation, have locations in over 30 different countries including facilities in Cambridge, Ontario. While McDonald’s has over 36,000 local restaurants in more than 100 countries serving nearly 75 burgers every second feeding 68 million people per day. (Lubin, 2012)

The large growth of trade throughout the world has come about as technology and communication improves, shipping becomes reliable and trade agreements have been negotiated. There are really six reasons that companies look to expand globally. (Lopers et al., 2016, p. 36) They are:

  • Reduce costs (labour, taxes, tariffs, etc.)
  • Improve the supply chain
  • Provide better goods and services
  • Understand markets
  • Learn to improve operations
  • Attract and retain global talent

Many businesses seek to reduce their costs by moving operations to take advantage of cheaper wages or less stringent government controls on operation practices (e.g. environmental controls, health and safety, etc.) or taxes and tariffs. In Mexico, maquiladoras (free trade zones) allows manufacturers to cut their costs of taxation by paying only on the value added by Mexican workers. (Rosenberg, 2010) Yes, it is important to reduce costs but remember from our previous unit, it is also important to do it ethically.

Improve the supply chain
Companies may go international to go where unique resources are available. For instance, Kitchener-Waterloo has become a hub of technology as its close to the University of Waterloo, Wilfrid Laurier and Conestoga College computer and engineering graduates. Or, the world’s leaders in perfume manufacturers are in Grasse, France.
Provide better goods and services
If a company moves close to its customers it can adjust the product or service to customize to meet the unique needs or wants in that area. McDonald’s serves wine in nice restaurants in France as food is important there—no fast food feel for the French. Or, have you noticed Tim Horton’s coffee or Coca-Cola tastes different in other countries? Locating in other countries also allows quicker access to that area’s customers.
Understand markets
When located in global markets companies can extend a product’s life. Introduction and phasing out of products or services vary across the world allowing companies to extend their sales life cycles. If close to a market, a company will be quicker to spot opportunities for products or services. Plus, products go through the Product Life Cycle at different speeds in different countries. Cell phone features vary widely between Japan and North America. Or, in developing countries with little internet or disposable income, personal computers may not be as “new” as here in Canada. It allows companies to smooth out their business cycles if different markets are demanding different products at different times.
Learn to improve operations
Locating in different countries also allows firms to learn new techniques. CAMI in Ingersoll, Ontario was originally formed as a joint venture between Suzuki and General Motors. General Motors wanted to learn more about Japanese auto assembly methods while General Motors could provide information about the North American labour and environmental laws to Suzuki.
Attract and retain global talent
By broadening the areas of operation, companies can locate, attain and retain more talented employees from a deeper, wider pool of possible employees. And, to retain the top flight employees, challenges and growth opportunities can be provided by having the flexibility of moving between countries experiencing high and low economic cycles.
