Operations Strategy in a Global Environment

Ethical Issues in a Global Market

While many other technological, political and market demands push firms towards globalization, many challenges remain.

One of the challenges is reconciling the differences in the social and cultural behaviours. What one country deems acceptable may be considered unacceptable or illegal in another. Bribery, child labour, polluting of the environment, protection of intellectual property, or discrimination against women managers are only a few where views between countries and cultures vary widely.

For managers today, balancing these ethical issues is tricky. Should a company invest in a country where they can realize lower overall costs but yet, might be criticized for participating in a country with less stringent labour or environmental laws?

Globalization is full of opportunities and risks but managers need to develop, then follow, their missions and strategies to move forward.
