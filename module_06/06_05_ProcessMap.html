Process Map

(To Organize the Data)

The next TQM tool that we would use for this example describes the steps in a process - Process Mapping.

In your text, read the example on page 97 and extra pages 112 to 114 (Lopers et al, 2016).

Process Mapping is an excellent way to understand the steps within a set boundary of a process then identify value-added activities and activities that need improvement or elimination. For us, we are going to look at the fast food delivery process. It is important to keep it simple. See the following for the basic symbols we will use.

Figure 1. Basic symbols for Process Mapping or Flowcharting

Notice in Figure 2 which follows, there is a start and an end to the process denoted by rounded corner boxes. This is the part of the process we want to examine. If not indicated we might wonder why it didn’t include preparing the food, etc. We want to concentrate on just the delivery part. The time for each step adds up to the cycle time.

Figure 2. Process chart to deliver the food

The cycle time is the total time for all the steps.

Note: we have assumed5 to 20 minutes for the driving part. Total cycle time = 28 to 48 minutes.
