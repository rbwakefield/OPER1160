Quality Management Tools

Tools of TQM

Notice how the charts below are broken into three categories: Generating Ideas, Organizing the Data, and Identifying Problems.

The charts you need to be able to produce are labelled below with the icon.

Tools for Generating Ideas

(a) Check Sheet: an organized method of recording data

(b) Scatter Diagram: a graph of the value of one variable vs. another variable

(c) Cause and Effect Diagram: A tool that identifies process elements that might effect an outcome

Tools for Organizing the Data

(d) Pareto Chart: A graph to identify and plot problems or defects in descending order of frequency

(e) Flow Charts (Process Diagrams): A chart that describes the steps in a process

Tools for Identifying Problems

(f) Histogram: A distribution showing the frequency of occurrences of a variable

(g) Statistical Process: A chart with time on the horizontal axis to plot values of a statistic

The ones we are concentrating on are the:

  1. Check Sheet to record your initial data.
  2. Pareto Charts to identify and plot problems or defects in descending order of frequency.
  3. Cause and Effect Diagram to identify causes of an effect or problem.
  4. Flow Chart or Process Mapping to describe the steps in a process and the associated cycle time.

A fifth one, a Statistical Process Control Chart, shows values over time and is used to identify problems in a process. You’re looking for what is within the upper and lower control limits. Watch for it being mentioned in the Darden Farms: Field to Fork” video but you are not responsible for producing one.
