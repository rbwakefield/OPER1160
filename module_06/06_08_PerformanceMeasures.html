Performance Measures – A Cautionary Note

Performance Measures are important tools to achieve better quality, continuously improve and manage processes.

However, it is important to also remember to look at a situation not just the facts. Remember the grocery shelf stocking productivity example in Unit 2 and how there were a lot more factors than just the “winning” team’s productivity? The same caution applies to this unit. Remember people react when they are being measured and they react to the measurements. They focus on the variables and behaviour being measured and spend less attention on those not being measured.

Ensure you’re measuring the right thing. In the supermarket checkout example should we measure just the cashier’s productivity or what about the shoppers’ satisfaction? Or, if a trucking company is rewarded for always being on time should we not stop and look at the inventory levels in the system? What if the company was stockpiling extra inventory close by to prevent any delays?

Quality control and performance measures are important in business.

Peter Drucker often stated the sentiment “If you can’t measure it you can’t manage it!”
