Supply Management

Summary and References

This was an interesting chapter that examined the Profit Leverage effect that showed money saved in purchasing goes straight to the bottom line. Then, we explored the various steps in the strategic sourcing process then moving through the P2P stages and examining 5 future trends.

The successful procurement of products/services is not possible without the co-operation of all partners in the supply chain. Selecting and managing the right partners is crucial to achieving cost, quality and performance targets. Firms that do not take advantage of global supply chain opportunities generally underperform against those firms that do. This is because “Global” firms are able to get better prices, quality and service because they can take advantage of structural differences between countries, like labour cost, proximity to raw materials or to distribution networks. Think back to unit 2 (week 3). As a result, “Global” firms are more competitive and have more visibility to world economic changes.

In the next unit we will review and expand on this material plus introduce you to the group case that centres on helping a business decide if they should outsource or maintain services internally.
