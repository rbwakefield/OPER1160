Supply Management

What is a Supply Chain?

Remember this diagram from Week 1 where we first defined it? Now we are getting more detailed. A supply chain is the integration of all the activities that procure materials and services, transform them into intermediate goods and services and final products and services, and delivers them through a distribution system. Goods, services, information and financials move throughout this chain. It requires close coordination up and down the chain (Lopers, Wilson, Hill, 2016, p. 7) The objective is to build a chain of suppliers that focuses on maximizing value to the end customer.

DID YOU KNOW?

In Canada, the purchasing profession is represented by two professional associations Supply Chain Management Association of Ontario (SMCAO) which offers a Certified Supply Chain Management Professional (S.C.M.P.) accreditation program and the Ontario Public Buyers Association (OPBA) which offers a couple of different certifications for public or professional buying.

Figure 1: Diagram depicting a Supply Chain. Adapted from Lopers et al., 2016, p 7

Notice the OEM and Tier suppliers’ labels on the supply chain diagram? OEM means Original Equipment Manufacturer. Tier 1 suppliers are direct suppliers to the OEM. Tier 3 would supply to Tier 2 which supplies to the Tier 1 company. For complex assemblies, each tier organizes the previous suppliers and pieces are added at each level before shipping to the OEM for final assembly.

Information Flows could be specifications, shipping, or billing information and moves up and down the supply chain. The more information, the better the supply chain’s interactions.

Cash Flows generally flow upstream as payment but could flow downstream as credits.
