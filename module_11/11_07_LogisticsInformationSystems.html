Logistics Information Systems

There are three main categories of logistics information systems:

1

Decision Support Tools

Decision support tools help logistic professionals make the best possible logistics decisions with the information they have at their disposal. Logistic solutions are expensive to implement so these tools help companies choose locations, predict costs, travel times or how decisions will impact other parts of the operation.

2

Execution
Systems

These systems help with specific activities like determining the load pattern on a container ship, plotting vehicle routings or developing a weekly schedule for deliveries.

3

Planning
Systems

These are the most detailed logistics information systems that take care of all the details. They could range from satellite tracking systems, to RFID tracking of shipments, to automating payment and billing systems. They help monitor and identify potential and developing problems.
