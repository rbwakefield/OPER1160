Different Modes of Transportation

There are five main categories of transportation modes and companies make their selection based on their operational needs.

Here is a summary of the strengths and weaknesses of each of them.

Water

High cost to build infrastructure with limited locations at deep water ports. Once built, the cost to run per kilo is the cheapest for a variety of products, especially bulky, low value or containerized finished goods shipments. Due to very slow transit times, long distances, and weather conditions, this method is the least reliable.

Air

Varied infrastructure costs as air shipments can use small or large equipment and facilities. Think of a helicopter landing on a pad or in a field versus a cargo jet landing at an international airport. This mode is best for shipping high value, low-volume, time sensitive goods due to its’ highest shipping cost and fastest delivery speed.

Road

This is by far the most accessible and versatile mode of transportation as it can be built anywhere and at a low cost if necessary (does not have to be a paved road). This mode is best for moving small, low value shipments to their final destination. It has limited capacity and high cost per kilo but is flexible and reliable.

Rail

Infrastructure costs are significant, but once built rail is an inexpensive method to move bulky cargo long distances over land. Due to limited accessibility, this mode is usually paired with water and/or road (multi-modal) to successfully move goods to their final destination. Due to this frequent switching with other modes, rail is not very reliable compared with most other modes.

Pipeline

This mode has the highest infrastructure cost but the lowest charge per kilo once operational. It is slow and is limited to low value liquid commodities. There is an option for in-transit storage when using pipeline.
