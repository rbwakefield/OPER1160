Operations and Productivity

Summary and References

Operations Management is one of the three major functions in any organization. We have come a long way in making improvements in operations in the twentieth century. The operations function creates goods and services by transforming inputs into outputs and by improving this conversion, using fewer inputs with the same outputs or creating more outputs with the same inputs, we improve our productivity, which then improves our standard of living. Although productivity improvements may be hard to achieve, especially in services, operations managers drive most of these improvement activities. In today’s world companies must also be ethical, practise social responsibility and aim for sustainability for our future generations. In the next unit, we will be looking at Operations Strategy in a Global Environment.
