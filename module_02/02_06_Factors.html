Operations and Productivity

Factors for Improved Productivity

Three factors that have been historically identified as critical to achieving improved productivity are labour, capital and management. Note the relative percentages. These indicate how much each one contributes to the annual increase. (Lopers et al., 2016, p. 18)

The Three Factors

Labour (10%)

Productivity can be improved by the labour inputs when the people are healthier, better-educated and better-fed. Many companies recognize this by giving educational grants and scholarships or providing subsidized or free meals for their employees.

Capital (38%)

Tools are important and capital investment can improve the tools we use.

Management (52%)

This is the key. Management can plan and combine the labour and capital through process improvements to effectively increase productivity.
