A team of 3 clerks stocked 24 shelves in an hour. What was their productivity?
= 24/3 = 8 shelves stocked per hour
A second team of 2 stocked 40 shelves in two hours. What was their productivity?
= 40 / (2 x 2) = 40/4 = 10 shelves stocked/hour
Who was more productive? What was the percentage difference in their productivity per hour?
= new-old/old = 10 – 8/8 = 2/8 or the second team is 25% more productive.

Points to Consider

Does this mean that Team 2 was more efficient?

No. Efficiency would be measured Actual Productivity / Standard Productivity.

Should you give Team 2 a bonus for being more productive?

Not necessarily… there might be other reasons they were more productive.

Think of three reasons Team 2 may have been more productive

Qualitative Considerations

Always think about qualitative considerations when examining productivity calculations. You need to measure “apples to apples”.
