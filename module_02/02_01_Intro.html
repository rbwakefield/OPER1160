Introduction

In this unit, we’ll look at the history of Operations Management, what they do today and what you might see in the future. We’ll move into distinguishing the differences between products and services then look at calculating single-factor productivity and what multi-factor productivity would look like.

It is also important to remember you can’t just use a productivity calculation so we’ll look at other measurements. Then, we’ll examine three factors that are critical to improve productivity and discuss some of the challenges to increase service productivity.

We’ll end by looking at the importance of ethics and corporate social responsibility in the field of Operations.

Week 2 Learning Outcomes

1.2 Describe the history of Operations Management and the current functions that operations managers perform.

1.3 Explain why the Operations Management function differs between goods and services.

1.5 Calculate productivity and explain other variables that factor into productivity calculations.

1.6 Explain the concept of business ethics and corporate social responsibility and provide examples using current events.
