Managing Inventory

Inventory Management Simulation

Objective

Manage stock of electronics device to minimize costs and maximize profits. Your Profit Goal is $1m.

Scenario

You are the store manager at a local branch of DigiLife, a large electronics retail chain. A new version of a popular consumer electronics device called the Amulet is coming out this year. It is your job to sell as many Amulets as you can while minimizing your costs in order to maximize your store’s profits.

Learning Objectives

  • Calculate Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) and Reorder Point.

Pointers

  • Use the EOQ formula = sqrt (2DS/h) and ROP formula = (D x L) + SS
  • D = Demand, S = Order Cost, h = Holding Cost, L = Lead Time, SS = Safety Stock
  • Using EOQ will help guide you towards better decisions about order size and the amount of time between orders
  • Understand that D is variable…you may be given an average demand but actual demand may fluctuate from day to day.
  • You need to watch carefully and not miss your calculated reorder point. Note, I added what I thought was adequate for safety stock
  • Leadtime is six days – 1 day to place order and 5 days shipping.
  • Ordering costs aren’t always obvious
  • Think about the economic impacts of defects and damage, stockouts and rush orders
  • EOQ will guide you…it might not be the exact answer but it will get you close
  • Remember you should try to never stock out. If you have a high enough reorder point, you should be able to do that!
  • Read all Reports and Memos before starting especially the cost analysis for ordering cost (S) and holding cost (H).
  • Start the game with an order of 500, observe the daily demand (D) over about 3 days and then calculate EOQ and ROP. The EOQ and ROP will change as the demand changes throughout the game.

Remember, you have 3 tries to perfect your inventory ordering.
