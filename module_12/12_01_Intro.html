Introduction

Inventory is a major investment and resource to businesses, even to service businesses. Think about a restaurant… if not managed well, inventory can be a major cost. Uses of inventory will drive different inventory decisions (manufacturing vs. service). Marketing and sales will need inventory to coincide with their plans and Customer expectations will drive different inventory decisions at different points in time.

We start by looking at the various roles and types of inventory plus what drives companies to keep inventory. We distinguish between independent and dependent demand, then look at the two main review systems; periodic and continuous. We then turn to calculating turns, the order quantity for periodic and continuous review systems. Since continuous review systems have a set quantity to order, we develop the formula and examine the "best" number for EOQ (economic order quantity). Marketing needs inventory to sell, production needs inventory to manufacture, maintenance needs inventory to fix things, accounting tracks it, and customers may be upset or simply leave if we don’t have.
