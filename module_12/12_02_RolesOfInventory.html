Managing Inventory

Roles of inventory in businesses

Raw Materials inventory may be used to produce items to sell to customers. Part-way through processing, inventory is called Work-in-process (WIP). When completed inventory will be called Finished Goods (to sell) or Service/Spare Parts (to fix or repair customer purchases). In addition, supporting activities like maintenance, repair or operating (MRO) inventory is needed to run the business.

Types of inventory

Cycle Stock

It is the most active type. Companies bring it in to sell and is it replenished cyclically when supplier orders are received.

Figure 1: Stock is sold, reordered, replenished… it “cycles” through.

Safety stock

The extra bit of inventory that a company holds “just in case” to protect again uncertainties of demand or replenishment receipt.

Anticipation inventory

Additional inventory, over and above regular cycle stock, to cover projected trends of increased sales, planned promotions, seasonal fluctuations, etc. For instance, plants, and dirt, etc. brought in in “anticipation” of spring planting season.

Hedge inventory

The extra inventory is purchased in case some event may happen. It will cost extra to carry this stock and the event may or may not happen so top management approval is often required. For example: The company may speculate there will be strike at its supplier so they bring in extra stock. Maybe they think there will be a price increase so they stock up in advance.

Transportation (in-transit) inventory

Inventory that is moving between points in the supply chain. Maybe it is coming from the supplier or maybe we have sent a consolidated shipment to another location.

Smoothing Inventory

Inventory that is used to make up the need when demand is higher than production levels.
