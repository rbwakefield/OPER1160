Managing Inventory

ABC Analysis of Inventory

To more effectively manage inventory, companies will often divide the inventory into chunks.

A,B,and C. It allows managers to focus on the critical ones (A-items) and not on the trivial many (C-items). Remember Pareto’s Principle or the 80/20 rule….its the same idea here….80% of the overall sales is based on only 20% of the items.

For ABC, it is similar but is usually…

A class:
10- 20% of the items represent 70-80% of the sales.
B class:
15 – 30% of the items accounts for 10 – 20% of the sales or usage.
C class:
Ebottom 40 - 50% of the items is usually only 5 - 10% of the sales.

Thus, A-items need tight inventory control and better sales forecasts. They will be examined often weekly or even daily with frequent reorders…avoiding stock-outs will be a priority.

C-items often are not reordered until the items on-hand are sold. This category is often examined for elimination from the inventory as not of much value for sales.

B-items are in the middle and are monitored to see if they are starting to move and should be moved up or should be eliminated.

Although sales volume is a big consideration, the importance to customers and the operations may also put items in the A-class.
