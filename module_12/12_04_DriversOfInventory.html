Managing Inventory

Drivers of Inventory

The drivers of inventory or the reasons companies maintain inventories are:

Uncertainty in Demand or Supply

Uncertainty of demand means we are not exactly sure when a customer will order stock. This independent demand means a company wants to have inventory onhand or the customer may go elsewhere which means a “lost sale”. Or, if the company expedites it they might pay extra. Or, they might discount it, or deliver it , etc to appease the customer.

Uncertainty of Supply means we keep stock as we are not sure of the reliability of the suppliers’ deliveries, or, if the price is about to rise and again, etc. we want to ensure the stock is on hand for our customers. We would hold cycle stock, that little extra safety stock or hedging inventory to offset possible interruptions in supply or price increases.

Mismatches in Demand and Process Volume

This inventory builds because the amount being sent is greater than the demand. This is due to the item being sent in a set quantity but a different quantity is required. If you need 2 bolts but they come in packages of 12, you would have 10 left in inventory. This cycle stock is the most common reason for inventory.

Mismatches in Demand and Capacity

This inventory builds because the amount being produced is different than the demand. Remember the Smoothing Inventory. Inventory is built up when the production is higher than what is produced.

Mismatches in Demand and Process Lead-time

When we go to the store, we expect items to be on hand but inventory might be coming from any of a number of suppliers or locations. Companies need to forecast what you’ll demand and determine what to order. For instance, they’ll order in anticipation inventory of Christmas trees as they forecast they will need them for December sales. If stock is ordered but still on its way it is transportation stock.

Independent vs dependent demand for stock

Independent demand is for finished goods or service parts and it is unpredictable in timing and quantity. It is forecasted and we might carry cycle, safety and anticipation inventory to help inventory stockouts.

Dependent demand is tied to production. If we are building a four-legged table and we know we are building 40 tables, we could calculate the dependent demand for 160 table legs.
