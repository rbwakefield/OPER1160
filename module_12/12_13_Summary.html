Managing Inventory

Summary and References

IIn this unit, we learned that the cost of different inventory decisions can dramatically affect a firm’s profitability. Understanding these costs, including the costs of “stock outs” due to no inventory when a customer wants a product/service, requires in-depth analysis. Operations has the responsibility to establish the company’s inventory policies and control inventories. Evaluating the right order technique for the situation can increase customer satisfaction and increase the company’s profitability.

In the next unit, we will look at the Business Planning Process which is the co-ordination mechanism that ensures all functional areas are working towards the goals and objectives of the company. The Business Planning Process flowchart is the cornerstone for many higher level Supply Chain and Operations Management courses. We will see the decisions made, the time frames covered and how each level is linked together in this flowchart.
