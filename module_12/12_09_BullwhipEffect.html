Managing Inventory

Bullwhip Effect

One of the effects in an inefficient supply chain is called the Bullwhip Effect.

It refers to the effect felt when a small change in demand downstream (customer), generates larger changes in the supply position upstream at the retailer, wholesaler, and manufacturer. See Figure 1 for an illustration of this effect. When a supplier upstream sees a larger order coming in, they may decide to order extra inventory to cover future large orders. This can ripple through the whole supply chain causing stockouts and excess inventory.

Reducing the Bullwhip Effect

The Bullwhip Effect can be reduced or even eliminated by synchronizing the supply chain as follows:

  • eliminate volume discounts so ordering is evener
  • reduce ordering costs so the customer doesn’t buy in bulk
  • have open and continuous communication up and down the supply chain creating an integrated process
