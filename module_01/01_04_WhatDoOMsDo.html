Introduction to Operations Management (OM)

What do Operations Managers Do?

Where are today’s Operations careers? Everywhere!

Often these are behind the scenes done by people that are doers, problem solvers or maybe hands on. These are the careers where you deal with customers, purchase supplies or services, plan and get projects done. These are the positions that keep an organization running smoothly and efficiently. Careers are many and varied. The popular website, Monster, explains “Operations” job titles (Monster, 2017). Additionally, have a look at this PDF for Facts and Figures from the Canadian Supply Chain Sector Council. (CSCSC, 2017).

Now, take a look at this chart from the Supply Chain Management Association showing many positions for this industry (SCMA, 2017). At the top (in yellow boxes) it shows the breadth of the sector and then below (in blue) you’ll see examples of specific jobs in the different areas. The numbers refer you to NOCs (National Occupational Classification codes) as noted at the bottom of the chart.

The Careers in the Supply Chain website describes the area of Supply Chain and Careers in this field (Careers in the Supply Chain, 2017).
