Welcome to OPER1160!

As one of the three major functions of business: Marketing, Operations, and Finance/Accounting, Operations Management exists in every business whether it is a retail store, a bank, a restaurant, a college, an airline, a hospital, an office, or a factory.

This course introduces the student to Supply Chain and Operations Management; how products and services are created and delivered to the customer. We’ll look at the design of goods and services, managing quality, supply chain management, logistics and inventory and the business planning process. Concepts and applications of operations management will be explored in a variety of business sectors.

This course has a lot of introductory terminology to understand. The concepts are many but you will find they tie into all of your studies of business and your everyday lives. We encourage you to look around at home, at school, at work – everywhere in your everyday lives to see how Operations applies.

Week 1 Learning Outcomes

1.1 Define Supply Chain and Operations Management, related industry associations, and explain reasons for studying them.

1.2 Describe the history of Operations Management and the current functions that operations managers perform.

1.4 Discuss the growth in services and current trends in Operations Management.

1.7 Interpret how the OM concepts in each unit are applicable to your program of study or functional area.
