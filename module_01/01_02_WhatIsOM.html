Introduction to Operations Management (OM)

What is OM?

Operations Management (OM) is a discipline. Often we mistakenly hear it is the “creation of goods and services”… but no, that’s production.

The definition of Operations Management is

“the set of activities that create value in the form of goods and services by transforming inputs into outputs”.

(Lopers, Wilson, Hill, 2016, p.5)

This could apply to a manufacturing plant like Toyota Motors, a restaurant like McDonald’s, a retail store, an office, or a landscaping and lawn cutting business.

What is a Supply Chain?

"A supply chain is the global network of organizations and activites that supplies a firm with goods and services”

(Lopers, Wilson, Hill, 2016, p.5)

Goods, services, information and financials move both ways throughout the supply chain. It requires close coordination up and down the chain. The objective is to build a chain of suppliers that focuses on maximizing value to the end customer. We’ll look at this in more detail in later in the course.
