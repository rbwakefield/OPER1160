Introduction to Operations Management (OM)

Assignment 1: Career Research

Overview

This assignment will be completed and submitted to the Assignment 1 drobox plus summarize your findings on the Discussion Board. Refer to the Assignment 1 rubric for more details.

Week Due: End of Week 3 – see the Instructional Plan.
Value of Assignment: 3%

Purpose

The purpose of this assignment is to learn more about “What is Operations?” and potential careers in the Supply Chain and Operations Management Field by researching career descriptions and a particular Operations Management or Supply Chain job advertisement. This ad will be limited to a current Canadian job position. This knowledge will help you relate to this functional area of a business with more credibility and understanding.

Instructions

Research a particular career from the Supply Chain or Operations Management field to find out more about the job responsibilities and salary expectations. The career title must come from the National Occupational Standards (NOC) for Supply Chain Roles provided by the Canadian Supply Chain Sector Council.

Ten Critical Decisions of Operations Management

  1. Design of goods and services
  2. Managing quality
  3. Process and capacity design
  4. Location strategy
  5. Layout strategy
  6. Human resources and job design
  7. Supply-chain management
  8. Inventory, material requirement planning, and JIT (just-in-time)
  9. Intermediate and short-term scheduling
  10. Maintenance

Note: You may find a job that does not have a NOC code, but does fall under one of the Ten Critical Decisions of Operations Management. If you find a job that doesn’t have a NOC code but fits into a category below, you may use it for your assignment.

Step One
Choose a career that interests you and look up the NOC code provided by the Canadian Supply Chain Sector Council.
Step Two
Find a current Canadian job posting for the career researched in Step 1. Your school’s job board, Monster.ca, local newspapers, etc. can be used.
Step Three
Research the salary expectations for the job posting in Step 2. (See below for details).
Step Four

Based on the above steps, write a report that includes:

  1. In the title on the first page, lists the chosen company name, the job title of the selected posting and its NOC code.
  2. In the first paragraph it gives a brief description of the chosen company and its main products and/or services.
  3. Describes a minimum of 8 job responsibilities and/or requirements of the career selected. Can be in point form.
  4. Identifies and explains 2 job responsibilities in the posting that you would find very interesting.
  5. Identifies and explains 2 job responsibilities that you would find not attractive.
  6. Identifies this job’s potential salaries taken from at least 3 sources. Present them in a table and ensure you have included references in the write up. Figure 1 shows a possible salary comparison table.

  7. Explain possible reasons for the similarities and/or differences in the salaries from these three sources?
  8. Is this a job that you would be interested in pursuing? Why or why not?

Step Five
Post a summary of your report findings to the Discussion Board: Career Research (Steps 1-4).

Formatting Requirements for the Paper Submission

  • Used Calibri font size 12 for the body of the report with 1 or 1.15 line spacing – not double-spaced please. Refer to the Assignment # 1 marking guide for more detail.
  • Put a title on your first page that includes the name of the company, the job title and NOC code. Ensure all pages are numbered and in the footer on each page, include your name and the name of the company and the job title. No title page is required.
  • The Originality Checker feature is turned on in the Assignment box and it is up to you to ensure you used your own words but also included intext citations to give authors credit for the gathered information.
  • Prepared a list of references for all sources utilized to prepare the paper. It goes at the end of your paper and is NOT included in your page count.
  • Spelling, and grammar are important.

Salary Expectations

What would be the salary range you would expect to earn in this position? It may be given but most job advertisements do not tell you this. We want you to find out an approximate salary range for this type of job from other similar job ads, and other industry, educational and government sites and sources. Use at least 3 sources for comparison. If a salary is quoted, is it competitive? Think about what factors might make it different?
