Concept development

This is the start of the process in developing new products or services. Ideas are plentiful and little to no money has yet been invested. Ideas are brought in externally from customers, suppliers, and internally from all departments; "What if we just… ?”

Planning

From there, ideas are vetted and examined for plausibility. Again, customers are asked for ideas on looks, functions, and what they might pay. Competitive analyses are done to see what is already being done and if a niche is available. Operations discuss possible suppliers and methods of producing it.

Design & development

If still being examined, plans are drawn, prototypes or initial models are tried. By this stage most of the decisions about products and processes have been made and the majority of the costs have been determined.

Commercial preparation

The plant or offices (for services) are geared up for test runs of production. They might be readying new lines, warehouses or offices. Training occurs and advertising is readied touting the product or service’s features. This stage will be certainly easier if the various dimensions of product or service design is considered. Are they building on their existing capabilities? Have they considered the costs both obvious and hidden? Can they produce enough to meet customer demands?

Launch

And finally—the launch. The product or service is advertised and sold to the customers. Operations are scrambling to keep the channels of distribution full and engineers are already gathering warranty and service information to add to customer requests for the next generation of the product. And then, the cycle or process starts again!
