Introduction

The purpose of this unit is to describe how product and service design affects operations and supply chain activities and what role different departments, including operations and supply chains, play in the product development process. The “Casting a Net Around Malaria” story at the beginning of the chapter (Lopers et al., 2016, p. 62) is good at illustrating how a good product concept is important but then producing and distributing the product to the customers can also present a significant challenge in the product and service development process.

Product design refers to the development of both services and physical products. They need to be designed in such a way that they can be created effectively. The product & service development process refers to the overall process of developing a concept, planning, designing and development, commercial preparation and finally the launch. It's important to remember that product/service development is a cross functional effort affecting all the departments and areas in a business.
