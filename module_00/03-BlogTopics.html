Blog Topics

The following sections contain the questions to guide your blog posts for your Weekly Self-Awareness and Employability Blog. Go to ePortfolio for technical instructions setting up your blog as a presentation in ePortfolio. The blog will allow students to keep track of their learning, insights, accomplishments, and challenges with course objectives throughout the term and to practice reflective writing skills. Through this process students will establish their career goals and objectives. The total value of this assignment is 10% of the final grade.

Week 1 – Your Career Journey Begins

Post due: By Sunday (11:59 p.m.) of week 1

Begin by creating an About Me section. Include your name, program, and year. Include your previous degree or diploma (if applicable) and if you are working in a part-time job currently.

Also answer one of the following questions and include it in your section:

  • What do you like most about Conestoga College?
  • What is your favourite thing to do when you are not working or at school?
  • If you could visit anywhere in the world, where would you visit and why?
  • If you could have lunch with anybody in the world, who would you have lunch with and why?

Next, write your first entry—a 100-200 word message to your fellow group members. Give this entry the title "Blog Entry #1 – Welcome Message" and answer the following questions in a single paragraph using full sentences.

  • Why did you choose to take GCM2000?
  • What do you like about working in groups?
  • What do you hope to achieve in the course?
  • What was your favorite course from your previous semester and why?
  • What you want to do once you have graduated from your program?

Week 2 – Self-Assessment

Post due: By Sunday (11:59 p.m.) of week 2

In your weekly blog, describe how the self-assessments you completed have provided you with further information and knowledge about your preferred career choices as well as how you plan to use this information in your career planning and job search.

Consider the following:

  • What assessments did you complete this week?
  • Did you find any of the assessments more compelling than the rest?
  • Do you think the results were accurate? What did they reveal about you?
  • How will you use this information going forward?

Week 3 – Occupational Research

Post due: By Sunday (11:59 p.m.) of week 3

For your blog this week, post the charts you completed as part of the Occupational Scavenger Hunt and answer the questions below to reflect on your experience:

  • What types of occupational information will you seek out in the future? Where are you likely to find those sources of information?
  • Did you learn anything about occupations of interest using the LRC and other resource indicated?
  • How will you know which occupations are a right fit for you? What criteria is especially important for you to consider?
  • Did you find any occupations that you think will be a good fit for you? Why?

Week 4 – Labour Market Trends

Post due: By Sunday (11:59 p.m.) of week 4

For your blog this week, answer the following questions:

  • What news, trends, forecasts, and economic indicators have you collected towards understanding relevant sector/industry trends and Labour Market Information?
  • Did you find the process of collecting this information difficult? Why?
  • Based on your research, do you expect changes to market conditions, new products or innovations in the next six months?

Week 5 – Uncovering Your Network

Post due: By Sunday (11:59 p.m.) of week 5

Research the networking opportunities available to you in your area. Then select and attend a networking event or meeting to learn more about what opportunities they hold for you.

Answer the following questions in your blog:

  • Which event did you attend?
  • What occurred when you were there?
  • How did the experience challenge you? In what ways did it benefit you?
  • How do you see using networking events going forward?

Week 6 – Developing Your Brand

Post due: By Sunday (11:59 p.m.) of week 6

For your blog this week:

  • Upload text of your elevator pitch and personal brand statement (from your Week 6 Worksheet).
  • Write a blog entry to discuss what you chose to include for your pitch and statement and how you plan to use these to help you arrive on a job and thrive once you’re there.

Week 7 – Job Search Tools

Post due: By Sunday (11:59 p.m.) of week 7

Imagine that you have created a strong set of job search tools that have landed you an interview in a job in your field. However, before going to the interview, the recruiter has asked that you write a statement for them that walks them through your résumé and emphasizes your transferable skills.

For your blog post this week:

  • Write a statement that responds to the recruiter’s request. How would you explain your résumé in a statement? How would you use your résumé to focus on transferable skills?

Hint: Focus on academic, professional, and extra-curricular experience that demonstrates what you have learned, and refer to your transferable skills from your Self-Assessment Summary.

Week 8 – Job Search Strategy

Post due: By Sunday (11:59 p.m.) of week 8

In your blog this week:

  • Develop and post a useful job search plan using at least three job search strategies, considering industry dynamics, and including a discussion of the research you must undertake to secure employment.

    Take some time to consider your requirements or targets for a future job such as location, job industry opportunities, and your personal timeline for landing a position, taking into consideration how much time you can actually dedicate to your search.

    Some examples of job search strategies include making networking connections through LinkedIn and Indeed.ca, joining Conestoga College Alumni Groups, setting up informational meetings with new contacts, using industry-specific job search sites, attending career fairs or company information sessions on-campus, etc.

  • After posting your plan in your weekly blog, discuss what challenges you expect to face in following your job search plan. Then discuss how you plan to overcome these challenges.

Week 9 – Interviews and Job Offers

Post due: By Sunday (11:59 p.m.) of week 9

For your weekly blog, complete the Post-Interview Self-Evaluation (you can find it at the end of “The Three Interview Stages” document), and reflect upon the following questions:

  • What was the mock interview process like for you? What did you do well? What do you want to improve?
  • Does your verbal and non-verbal communication require any fine tuning? What makes you think so?
  • Did you find any of the questions difficult to answer? Why or why not?
  • Why do employers like to use behavioral questions?
  • Finally, describe your experience using InterviewStream for this week’s assignment.

Week 10 – Thriving in Organizations

Post due: By Sunday (11:59 p.m.) of week 10

For your weekly blog, answer the following questions:

  • Which responsibilities do you have as a worker to understand and comply with workplace regulations and legislation?
  • How are activities such as task allocation, coordination and supervision related to the achievement of organizational aims?
  • What is the relationship between organizational structure and organizational culture?
  • Which types of organizational structure and culture do you prefer?

Week 11 – Communicating with Others

Post due: By Sunday (11:59 p.m.) of week 11

For your weekly blog, reflect upon the following questions related to culture and communications:

  • Workplaces often possess different and unusual customs depending on the industry and location. Consider your industry in Canada. What aspects of workplace culture do you find challenging in this industry? How do you intend to deal with this challenge?
  • Think of a time when you experienced a breakdown in communication. Why was that? What could you do differently to improve communications?
  • How do you see yourself in terms of understanding intercultural communication issues? Would you consider yourself to be high in intercultural intelligence skills? Why?

Week 12 – Working with Others

Post due: By Sunday (11:59 p.m.) of week 12

For your weekly blog, discuss the workplace skills you have discovered this week by answering the following questions:

  • How do Canadian employers leverage diversity? How have you encountered diversity in your work and school in Canada?
  • Acting ethically means making the right choices. Have you encountered an ethical dilemma before? How did you respond to this dilemma?
  • How has your understanding of effective teamwork shaped your learning experience? What makes you a good team member? What can you do to be a better team member?
  • What are the differences between interpersonal and intrapersonal skills? What do you plan to do to improve these skills? Between the two, which area requires more development for you to thrive in the Canadian workplace?

Week 13 – Lifelong Learning

Post due: By Sunday (11:59 p.m.) of week 13

For your weekly blog, reflect upon your learning in the course by answering the following questions:

  • What are your expectations for “Arriving” on your career journey? Have these expectations changed since you started the course? What job search techniques still need to be improved for you to maximize your chances for success?
  • What do you plan to do to “thrive” in the Canadian workplace?
  • What will you do to continue your journey of lifelong learning? Do you have plans to develop new skills, refine your abilities, or move towards a new goal?
