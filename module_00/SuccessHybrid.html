How to Succeed in a Hybrid Course

A hybrid course combines online and classroom learning. The following topics provide some suggestions on how to be successful at learning in this course. Click on any of the headings to get more information.

What will I be able to do by the end of the course?

Successful completion of the course will enable the student to:

  1. Identify one’s strategic advantage in the marketplace by taking stock of personal style, skills, knowledge, interests, and values.
  2. Analyze historical and contemporary trends in Canada and their impact on the Canadian labour force to support career adaptability.
  3. Establish career goals and objectives to help target the job search on specific occupations and industries.
  4. Apply professional career management tools, techniques, and strategies, using current technologies, to position oneself as an ideal candidate with targeted employers.
  5. Build career workplace survival skills through an analysis of Canadian business organizational structures, legislation, cultures, behaviours, and ethics.
  6. Develop workplace communication, teamwork, and self-management skills that reflect best practice, cultural awareness, and an appreciation of working in the diverse Canadian labour force to support workplace integration.
  7. Evaluate personal and professional growth through reflective practice to make informed decisions regarding career directions.

How is this course delivered?

This course consists of 3 hours of online learning and 3 hours in the classroom and 9 hours both before and after class completing research, assignments, and other learning activities all designed to support your success in this course as well as in your future career. The classroom portion of this class consists of presentations from guest speakers, research activities, games, and a variety of other active learning exercises. You will complete the online learning for the week before coming to class. The online component of this course consists of readings, self-directed lessons, knowledge checks, worksheets, weekly blog postings, and discussions with the participants in the class. Both the classroom and online sections of the course work together to provide a comprehensive learning experience. Participation is expected in all elements of the course.

What does a hybrid course look like?

One advantage to a hybrid course is that you can do the online portion of the learning at whatever time fits your schedule. At the same time, you need to give yourself enough time to review and digest the content before going to class.

In general with online content, you should:

  • Access your course regularly (at least once a day, but no less than every 48 hours). Any changes to your course will be communicated through the online course (not by telephone or mail).
  • Be self-motivated. You will need to manage time effectively because you control when you work on the course.
  • Use the technology properly. Review available training modules in Help & Guides and ask for help when needed.
  • Be willing and able to commit the time necessary to successfully complete the course. Time commitments vary from course to course.
  • Ask questions if you do not understand.

What preparation is required for the classroom?

You will need to read through and complete all online activities prior to attending class. Some weeks, you will be required to bring worksheets that you have completed (or partially completed) during the online portion to class. The checklist at the end of each week will remind you what to bring to the in-class sessions. Requirements will vary from week to week.

How will I be evaluated in this course?

In order to receive credit in this course you must earn a final mark of at least 65%. You can learn more about the evaluation for this course in the Evaluations module. Start here with Evaluations at a Glance..

What do I do if I can’t meet a submission deadline?

Late submissions will be penalized at a rate of -5% per day.

What are the expectations for written assignments?

All written assignments consist of tasks which will be useful for you as a teacher, such as lesson plan summaries, learning module plans, and assessment tools. Any references cited as part of your assignments will follow APA style. Assignments containing plagiarism and/or missing proper documentation may be levied further penalties and deductions from those indicated on assignment rubrics.
