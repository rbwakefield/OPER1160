How is this course delivered?

This course consists of a one hour active learning laboratory and two hours of online learning. The active learning laboratory consists of group work, activities and interactive discussions. You must come prepared for each weekly active learning lab.

The online component of this course consists of self-directed lessons much like the work you are doing right now and interactive and collaborative work such as discussion postings and written reflections. The most important feature is that much of the work in this course involves working with others and building a supportive and caring campus community.

Special Scheduling – Class and Lab

Anyone using the lab must have completed training in Fire Safety, AODA, and WHMIS. You can find information on this in the Course Information module. Click on the Mandatory training.

Appropriate dress must be worn. Failing to comply with this dress code will result in the person being asked to leave the lab area.

  • White lab coat or scrubs
  • Closed toe, closed heel, rubber-soled shoes
  • Hair tied back. If hair is long, then it must be up
  • No rings, necklaces or dangling earrings
  • A pair of studs may be worn, one in each ear
  • A plain wedding band may be worn
  • Nails should be kept neat and short; artificial nails and nail polish are not allowed
  • No hats to be worn (exceptions related to the Human Rights Code)
  • No denim or sweat pants
  • Nametags must be worn. Nametags can be obtained in the bookstore

Bags and coats cannot be brought into the lab.
